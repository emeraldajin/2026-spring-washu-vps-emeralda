The New Turing Test

(By Elan Ullendorff)

Review

I think the Turing Test Reading presents a really interesting way to view AI usage and art. I distinctively remember in one
of my high school art classes there was a debate about what was considered "art." Was a piece of driftwood picked out from
a river considered art? If art was evaluated by effort, then the driftwood wouldn't be considered art. However, if art was
qualified by intention, then the driftwood would count. The textbook concluded that it was the intent that made art, but
that was long before AI-generated content was produced. I feel like the reading proposed a new and updated alternative to
what qualifies as art by its expression. Art is about human expression so it would make sense to measure artistry by its
expressiveness, and in relation, humanity.
