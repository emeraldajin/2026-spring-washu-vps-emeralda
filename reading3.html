Reading

Cyborgs vs rooms, two visions for the future of computing

(By Matt Webb)

Review

Matt Webb's blog presented an interesting reframing of cyborgs as not just some unforeseeable future but rather part of our
reality by listing everyday examples such as Airpods (and their transparency mode). Somaforming seems less destructive to the
environment and more adaptive, but to gain technology that allows somaforming, we will exert some kind of force on the
environment. Still, it seems better than terraforming, especially when so much of our everyday environment has been changed.
I believe there must be a harmony/compatibility between the two. We shouldn't somaform to the point that we are out of touch
with our environment, nor should we terraform to the point that we destroy the environment we were made for.
